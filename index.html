About
From the ward to data science

I'm a health data scientist who began my career at the hospital bedside as a registered nurse in Ghana, and now turn health data into decisions. Over more than a decade across clinical, government and academic settings, I've built and managed health information systems, led monitoring and evaluation, and trained over 500 health professionals in data analysis and digital health tools.

I work day to day in Python, R, SQL, Power BI, Tableau and QGIS, and my applied research has contributed to more than 20 peer-reviewed publications in journals including PLoS ONE and BMJ. I'm currently based in Olomouc, Czech Republic, pursuing a European Joint Masters in Digital Society, Social Innovation & Global Citizenship, and I'm driven by one goal: making data genuinely useful for public health, and for the people behind the numbers.
